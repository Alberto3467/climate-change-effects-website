Introduction

Climate change is one of the most pressing global challenges of our time. It affects ecosystems, weather systems, and natural resources that support economic activities around the world. Rising global temperatures increase the frequency of extreme weather events such as floods, droughts, and melting polar ice caps.

When ecosystems are disrupted, the economic sectors that depend on them also suffer. Agriculture, fisheries, transportation, and infrastructure are vulnerable to environmental changes. These impacts can lead to financial losses, food insecurity, unemployment, and damage to communities and national economies.

Understanding these challenges encourages individuals to reflect on their role in protecting the environment and contributing to solutions that promote sustainable development and responsible stewardship of the Earth.

Economic Problems Caused by Climate Change

• Damage to infrastructure such as roads, bridges, and buildings.

• Agricultural losses caused by drought and changing weather patterns.

• Increased costs for disaster recovery and rebuilding.

• Job losses in industries like agriculture, tourism, and fisheries.

Solutions

• Transitioning to renewable energy sources.

• Supporting sustainable agriculture.

• Reducing greenhouse gas emissions.

• Promoting climate awareness and responsible consumption.

Responsible Life Planning and Christian Values

Responsible life planning encourages individuals to discover how their talents and interests can contribute to solving environmental and social challenges. For Christians, protecting the environment reflects the value of stewardship of creation and responsibility toward society.

Visible Effects of Climate Change on the Ecosystem and Economy

Flooding and Infrastructure Damage

Heavy rainfall and flooding caused by climate change can destroy roads, bridges, and transportation systems. When infrastructure is damaged, business operations stop, transportation becomes difficult, and governments must spend large amounts on rebuilding.

Drought and Agricultural Loss

Severe droughts reduce crop production and threaten food security. Farmers experience financial losses while food prices increase, affecting both local and global economies.

Melting Polar Ice Caps

Rising global temperatures melt polar ice caps, causing sea levels to rise. Coastal communities, fisheries, and tourism industries face serious risks from flooding and environmental disruption.
